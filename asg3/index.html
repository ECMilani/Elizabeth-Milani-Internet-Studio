Better User Experience Through Storytelling

As a writer myself, this article really connected with me. A big part of how I plan stories is to go in knowing not just how a story will end (in terms of a UX project, this could translate to knowing the final product) but also knowing how it will begin, and what will happen in the middle. Having a hyper-specific plan can help refine not only ideas, but the process of executing them.

Additionally, making a project hinge around emotion, the way most narratives do, will inevitably bleed through to the user. If the creator(s) could put passion and emotion into a project, it'll shine through in the final project. This can also help the user themselves form emotions regarding the final product. If one thinks of a website as essentially a choose-your-own-adventure story, then every story option needs to be both engaging, simple to navigate to, and most importantly, not boring!
