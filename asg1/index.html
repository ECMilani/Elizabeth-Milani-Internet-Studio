Reading

From this week's reading material, I learned a lot about the early web:

  1. The early web was more static and less interactive than today's Internet. It was essentially a digital magazine, with pictures and articles that could be read, but without the ability to do things like leave comments or interact with a site beyond viewing it.
  2. Coders have been using "hacks", such as a single-pixel gif to create space on a page, since practically the invention of html.
  3. The use of personal sites over social media is making a comeback today, as people are beginning to prefer the privacy and the ability to own their own data.


Semester Project Brainstorming

I've been looking for a good website to manage a to-do list on recently, and nothing has been able to meet my exact needs. What I'm looking for is a site where I can:

  • Assign subtasks to tasks. (For example, "do reading" would be a subtask of this week's "do homework" task.
  • Assign priority to tasks - between urgent and non-urgent tasks, and things I need to do vs. things I want to do.
  • Assign tasks over a period of time. (For example, I need to finish this homework assignment any time this week.)
  • Allow me to set up a reward system for myself for completing a certain number/percentage of tasks. (For example: if I do all my homework this week, I can go buy a cookie from Insomnia!)

This is something I believe would help not just me, but also others who struggle to get things done and make to-do list. Additionally, the rewards element adds room for business promotion via in-site special offers.
