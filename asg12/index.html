Recommended Movies

I chose a movie from each of the twelve main movie genres. Incidentally, most of these movies are rated R, because I love love love violence. And corruption arcs. And unhappy endings. And assassins.

Black Swan

Black Swan is a thriller about a young ballerina who yearns to become a star. When her company puts on a production of Swan Lake, our heroine manages to land the lead role, but while she plays the part of the White Swan beautifully, she struggles to embody the darkness of the Black Swan.

The Descent

The Descent is one of the most terrifying horror films I've ever watched. The story follows a group of women who get lost in an unexplored cave. The interpersonal strife between the characters combined with the claustrophobia and desperation makes for a truly terrifying film.

Kate

Kate is an action movie that follows a fatally-poisoned assassin with hours left to live seeking to avenge her own murder while also protecting a young girl. It's gorey, but also visually gorgeous.

The Substance

The Substance is a sci-fi horror that imagines a technology that allows people to live a double life - alternating weeks between being themselves and being a younger, better version of themselves.

The Crow

The Crow is a fantasy film. When a murdered rock star is supernaturally brought back from the dead, he sets out on a quest of violent revenge against his killers.

Serenity

Serenity is a space western about a ragtag group of misfits trying to protect two runaways. It's funny, action-packed, and has one of my favorite fight scenes of all time.

Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind

Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind is a romance/breakup movie that asks the question: if you could erase all memories of someone who broke your heart, would you? Not only is it a great, wonderfully psychological movie, it also inspired my favorite song of all time - "Eternal Sunshine" by Flower Face.

Shaun of the Dead

Shaun of the Dead is zom-com (zombie-comedy) about a great of friends attempting to survive the zombie apocalypse by getting wasted at their favorite bar. It's hilarious, and also, was the first R-rated movie I ever watched.

Donnie Darko

Donnie Darko is a bizarre drama film chronicling a series of wacky, disturbing events that follow a teenage boy's near-death experience. It's hilarious and overdramatic and creepy, all at once.

Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon

Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon is a wuxia martial arts movie. Without wanting to spoil two much, the film follows two warriors attempting to track down a mysterious thief who has stolen a powerful sword.

Memento

Memento is a mystery film with a main character who attempts to solve the mystery of his wife's murder while being unable to form new memories. It's topsy and turvy in true Christopher Nolan style, and it's got a thrilling reveal when the mystery is finally solved.

Rubber

Rubber is a movie that transcends genre. It must be experienced to be understood. A killer tyre ventures across the desert, committing telekinetic murders while an exclusive group is granted the privilege to watch from a distance.
